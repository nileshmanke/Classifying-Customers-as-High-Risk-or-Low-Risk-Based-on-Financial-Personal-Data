Introduction

In the modern banking sector, accurately assessing a customer's ability to repay a loan is critical for maintaining financial stability and minimizing losses. This project develops a sophisticated Bank Loan Repayment Risk Model designed to classify customers as either 'High Risk' or 'Low Risk' based on their personal and financial data.

By leveraging advanced Machine Learning algorithms, the model analyzes key predictors such as Credit Score, Debt-to-Income Ratio, and past financial history to provide real-time risk assessment.

Dataset Description

The project utilizes a dataset of 30,000 banking customers, containing a comprehensive range of financial and demographic attributes:

Age Annual Income Credit Score Employment Status Debt-to-Income Ratio Loan Amount Loan Term Past Loans Defaults Marital Status Education Level

* The dataset was pre-processed to handle categorical variables and feature scaling for optimal model performance.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Key insights derived from the initial data analysis revealed that:

  • Credit Score is the strongest predictor of loan default risk.
  • Low-income individuals with high debt-to-income ratios exhibit significantly higher risk flags.
  • The target variable (Risk_Flag) shows a balanced distribution for effective training.

Model Development & Evaluation

We implemented and compared multiple Classification models to identify the most accurate predictor. The final model utilizes an Ensemble approach for robust results.

Conclusion

The Bank Loan Repayment Risk Model demonstrates the power of machine learning in streamlining financial decision-making. By automating the credit risk assessment process, banks can improve efficiency, reduce human error, and proactively manage their loan portfolios.

Future enhancements could include incorporating alternative data sources like utility bill payments and social behavior patterns to further refine the prediction accuracy.
